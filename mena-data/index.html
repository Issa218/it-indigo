Country Snapshots

Palestine — Gaza Strip and West Bank

Crisis Level: CRITICAL

Since October 7, 2023, the Gaza Strip has experienced one of the most severe humanitarian collapses in modern history. Over 90% of Gaza's 2.3 million residents have been displaced — many multiple times. The near-total destruction of civilian infrastructure, a sustained military campaign, and a near-complete blockade of humanitarian aid have pushed the population to the edge of survival. In the West Bank, violence and settler attacks have intensified simultaneously.

  • 70,000+ Palestinians reportedly killed in Gaza (Oct 2023–2025)
  • 1.9 million displaced in Gaza (~90% of total population)
  • 61% of health facilities non-functional
  • 93% of school buildings damaged or destroyed (526 of 564)
  • UNRWA banned from bringing aid into Gaza since March 2, 2025

Syria

Crisis Level: SERIOUS

Syria's 14-year civil war entered a new phase after the fall of the Assad government in December 2024. Over 1.2 million Syrian refugees have returned since then, but conditions remain dire: 14.56 million face food insecurity, 38% of hospitals are partially or fully non-functional, and violence persists in coastal and northeastern regions.

  • 16.5 million people in need of humanitarian assistance (2025)
  • 7.4 million still internally displaced inside Syria
  • 4.5 million Syrian refugees registered in neighboring countries
  • 14.56 million people face food insecurity
  • 1.2 million refugees returned after Assad's fall (Dec 2024 onward)
  • 317 killed by landmines and unexploded ordnance since December 2024, including 71 children

Lebanon

Crisis Level: ELEVATED

Lebanon faces a compounding catastrophe: one of the worst economic collapses in modern history since 2019, the aftermath of massive conflict damage from 2024 fighting, and the weight of hosting one of the world's largest refugee populations per capita. Over 1.5 million Syrian refugees and 180,000 Palestinian refugees live within its borders.

  • 4.1 million people in need of humanitarian assistance (2025)
  • 4,400+ killed in Lebanon since October 2023
  • ~40% cumulative GDP decline since 2019
  • $14 billion in estimated conflict damage and losses (2024)
  • 1.5 million Syrian refugees hosted in Lebanon
  • ~1 million Lebanese face food insecurity
  • Lebanon hosts the highest refugee-to-population ratio of any country globally
