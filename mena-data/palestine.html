Background and Context

The Palestinian people have lived under Israeli military occupation since 1967. The Gaza Strip — a 365 km² coastal territory home to approximately 2.3 million people — has been under an Israeli and Egyptian blockade since 2007, following Hamas's takeover of the territory. This blockade severely restricted the movement of people and goods, limiting access to construction materials, medicine, fuel, and clean water for nearly two decades.

Palestinian refugees — descendants of the approximately 700,000 Palestinians who fled or were expelled during the 1948 Arab-Israeli war (known in Arabic as the Nakba, or "catastrophe") — constitute the majority of Gaza's population. UNRWA, the UN agency created to serve Palestinian refugees, has been the primary provider of education, healthcare, and relief services in Gaza for over 75 years.

On October 7, 2023, Hamas launched a large-scale attack on southern Israel, killing approximately 1,200 people and taking roughly 250 hostages. Israel responded with an intensive air, land, and sea military campaign on Gaza. Hospitals were destroyed, food supplies cut off, displacement became near-total, and humanitarian aid access was denied on a systematic basis.

A ceasefire came into effect on January 19, 2025, temporarily allowing limited aid movement and a partial exchange of hostages and prisoners. It collapsed on March 18, 2025, and full hostilities resumed. By mid-2025, UNRWA had been formally barred from bringing any aid into Gaza, leaving warehouses in Egypt and Jordan holding food for 1.1 million people blocked from entry.

Current Humanitarian Conditions (2025)

The situation in Gaza has been described by UN officials as catastrophic and without modern precedent. With 86% of Gaza under military zones or evacuation orders, civilians are compressed into a shrinking area with no reliable access to food, water, or healthcare. Famine conditions have been documented across parts of Gaza. Acute malnutrition is at ten times pre-war levels. Only 4 of 22 UNRWA health centers remain operational. Of 564 school buildings, 526 have been damaged or destroyed.

The economic shock has been severe. The Consumer Price Index in Gaza rose by 283% between October 2023 and September 2024. Over 660,000 children have been out of formal education for three consecutive school years — a generational learning loss with long-term consequences.

In the West Bank, Israeli forces launched "Operation Iron Wall" in January 2025, displacing over 32,000 Palestinian refugees from the Jenin, Tulkarm, and Nur Shams refugee camps. More than 1,000 settler attacks were documented in 2025. Since October 2023, at least 1,043 Palestinians have been killed in the West Bank, including at least 225 children.

Humanitarian Needs by Sector

Food Security

IPC Phase 4–5 conditions (Emergency to Famine) are documented across Gaza. The Consumer Price Index rose 283% between October 2023 and September 2024. Acute malnutrition is at ten times pre-war levels, with children under five and pregnant and nursing women among the most severely affected. The aid blockade prevents WFP and UNRWA from restocking food supplies.

Assessment: Critical

Healthcare

61% of all health facilities are non-functional. Only 4 of 22 UNRWA health centers remain operational. 18 hospitals are only partially functional, lacking electricity, surgical supplies, and the capacity to treat patients. There are severe shortages of anesthesia, antibiotics, blood, and generator fuel. Thousands of patients with chronic conditions are unable to access treatment.

Assessment: Critical

Shelter

An estimated 42,000+ tents used by displaced civilians have been damaged or destroyed in subsequent military operations. Most of Gaza's residential housing stock is uninhabitable. Displaced families have been forced to move repeatedly as evacuation orders expand across the territory.

Assessment: Critical

Education

93% of school buildings — 526 of 564 — are damaged or destroyed. Over 660,000 children have been out of formal education for three consecutive school years. UNRWA schools previously served as emergency shelters and are no longer operational in that capacity. The long-term human capital cost of this education crisis is immense.

Assessment: Critical

Water, Sanitation and Hygiene

Gaza's water and sanitation infrastructure is severely degraded. Desalination plants have been repeatedly damaged or shut down due to lack of fuel. Sewage systems are failing, raising the risk of cholera and other waterborne disease outbreaks. Access to clean water per person per day is a fraction of WHO minimum standards.

Assessment: Critical

Crisis Timeline

  1. Al-Nakba (The Catastrophe)

    1948 — Al-Nakba (The Catastrophe)

    Approximately 700,000 Palestinians fled or were expelled during the Arab-Israeli War. UNRWA was established in 1949 specifically to provide relief and services to Palestinian refugees.

  2. Israeli Military Occupation Begins

    1967 — Israeli Military Occupation Begins

    Following the Six-Day War, Israel occupied the West Bank and Gaza Strip — a military occupation that international law recognizes as ongoing to the present day.

  3. Oslo Accords

    1993 — Oslo Accords

    Israel and the PLO signed the Oslo Accords, establishing the Palestinian Authority and a framework for a two-state solution. A final status agreement has never been reached, and Israeli settlement construction in the West Bank has continued throughout.

  4. Gaza Blockade Imposed

    2007 — Gaza Blockade Imposed

    Following Hamas's military takeover of Gaza, Israel and Egypt imposed a comprehensive land, sea, and air blockade, severely restricting access to goods and people for over 15 years.

  5. Israeli Military Campaign

    October 7, 2023 — Israeli Military Campaign

    Israel launched a comprehensive military campaign on Gaza, beginning one of the most destructive military operations in the region's modern history.

  6. Ceasefire Agreement

    January 19, 2025 — Ceasefire Agreement

    A ceasefire came into effect, briefly allowing some aid movement and a partial hostage-prisoner exchange. Humanitarian organizations began documenting the full scale of destruction.

  7. UNRWA Aid Blockade

    March 2, 2025 — UNRWA Aid Blockade

    UNRWA formally barred from bringing humanitarian aid into Gaza. Warehouses holding food for 1.1 million people and flour for 2.1 million people remain blocked from entry.

  8. March 18, 2025 — Ceasefire Collapses

    Full-scale hostilities resume across Gaza. Casualty figures and displacement continue to rise.

Aid Response and Funding

The 2025 OCHA-coordinated Humanitarian Response Plan for the occupied Palestinian territory requests over $3 billion in funding. As of mid-2025, approximately 40% of the requested amount has been received, leaving a significant gap. UNRWA requires approximately $1.2 billion annually to operate and has also faced severe funding shortfalls following the withholding of contributions by several donor governments.
